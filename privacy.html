4. What Personal Data We Collect

4.1 Data You Provide Directly

  • Identity data: full name, farm name, email address, phone number
  • Location data: country, county, and region of your farm
  • Crop data: primary crop type, farm size, and number of active fields
  • Account credentials: your Pilot Access Code and app login details
  • Feedback data: weekly feedback forms, monthly validation sheets, and exit survey responses
  • Communications: emails, WhatsApp messages, and support call notes

4.2 Data Collected Automatically

  • Scan images: photographs of crops captured through the in-app scanner
  • GPS coordinates: the location of each scan, if you have granted location permission
  • AI interaction data: each diagnosis result, confidence score, and the scan that generated it
  • Treatment logs: treatments applied and outcomes recorded in the app
  • Device data: Android device model, operating system version, and app version
  • Usage data: session frequency, feature interactions, scan counts, and time spent in the app
  • Crash and error logs: technical logs generated when the app encounters an error

4.3 Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect payment or financial information during the Pilot — the app is free to use
  • We do not collect data from your contacts, messages, or other apps on your device
  • We do not collect biometric data such as fingerprints or facial recognition
  • We do not knowingly collect data from children under the age of 18

6. Crop Scan Images and AI Model Training

When you scan a crop using the Cropaite app, the photograph is processed by our AI model to generate a disease diagnosis. That image — along with the diagnosis result and treatment outcome (if logged) — is stored on Cropaite's secure cloud servers and used to train and improve the AI model.

  • Scan images are linked to your account for quality control purposes during the Pilot but are anonymised before being added to the training dataset.
  • You may request deletion of your scan images at any time by contacting privacy@cropaite.com. Note: images already incorporated into AI model weights cannot be individually extracted from the model, but they will be deleted from identifiable storage.
  • We do not use scan images to identify individual people. Our AI analyses plant material only.
  • Scan images are never shared with third parties in identifiable form without your explicit consent.

7. Location Data

The Cropaite app will request permission to access your device GPS when you perform a scan. Your exact location coordinates are recorded alongside each scan to build a regional disease prevalence map, power the agrovet locator, and improve AI accuracy by geographic zone. You may decline GPS permission without affecting the core scanning functionality.

8. Data Storage and Security

8.1 Where Your Data Is Stored

All Cropaite data is stored on secure cloud infrastructure hosted within Africa or the European Economic Area. We do not store personal data on servers located in countries without adequate data protection frameworks.

8.2 Security Measures

  • Encryption at rest: all stored data is encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • Encryption in transit: all data transmitted between your device and our servers uses TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Access control: only authorised Cropaite staff and vetted contractors may access personal data
  • Audit logging: all access to personal data is logged and reviewed regularly

8.3 Data Breach Response

In the event of a personal data breach, Cropaite will notify the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner of Kenya within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, and will notify affected participants without undue delay, as required by the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019.

10. Sharing Your Data

Cropaite does not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party. We share data only in the following limited circumstances:

10.1 Service Providers

We share data with trusted third-party service providers who help us operate the platform. These providers are bound by data processing agreements. Current categories include cloud infrastructure and hosting providers (Google Firebase, Render), AI analysis engine (OpenAI — processes images for analysis only), and mobile analytics tools.

10.2 Research Partners

Aggregate and anonymised data — with no information that could identify you or your farm — may be shared with agronomic research institutions for the purpose of improving crop disease knowledge across East Africa.

10.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your data if required to do so by Kenyan law, court order, or a competent regulatory authority. Where legally permitted, we will notify you before complying with such a requirement.
